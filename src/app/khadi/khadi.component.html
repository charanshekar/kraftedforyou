The Story Behind it all
The art of Ajrakh printing has been around since the 15th century. The craft of Ajrakh hand-block printing and natural dyeing, is more than 4000 years old and is practiced by local communities of Kutch and Sindhâ€¦ These textiles are distinguished by jewel-like colors with geometric, floral and architectural patterns. The cloth is made in a 16-step process and requires a high level of skill and concentration. From an early age, people in these local communities learn and enjoy the process of Ajrakh art while placing importance in engaging with the craft and with the person/designer, rather than on mere end transactional value. For them, Ajrakh is a way of life.
Sustainable. Natural. Eco-friendly.
Ajrakh is a form of traditional hand-block printing and resist dyeing using exclusively natural dyes and natural mordants. The intricate patterns of indigenous motifs captured on fabric make this craft admirable. The process is extremely laborious and complex, and practised by master artisans of Kutch. Every Ajrakh textile is carefully crafted, involving several steps by hand in its making. Each colour is extracted from nature. Blue is derived from Indigo. Red from madder. Dark greens and yellows from pomegranate skins and turmeric. Browns from Rhubab root. Purple from logwood. Black from scrap iron. And many more colours of nature. Patience is the message scrawled on each Ajrakh textile. Characterized by its lightweight and slightly shiny appearance, it is a fixture of bridal, evening wear and even daily wear. The softness & lightness of the fabric soothes you, while balancing the intricate Ajrakh motifs to give you a rich and elegant look.
